# Visualizations Developed:
1. Attrition by Gender

Objective

Analyze employee attrition by gender.

Insight

Male employees contribute a higher proportion of employee attrition compared to female employees.

2. Attrition by Age Group

Objective

Identify which age groups experience the highest employee attrition.

Insight

Employees in the younger and mid-career age groups show comparatively higher attrition.

3. Attrition by Department

Objective

Analyze employee attrition across departments.

Insight

Research & Development experiences the highest employee attrition.

4. Attrition by Job Role

Objective

Identify job roles with higher employee turnover.

Insight

Sales Executive and Laboratory Technician record the highest attrition.

5. Monthly Income vs Attrition

Objective

Study the relationship between salary and employee attrition.

Insight

Employees with lower monthly income tend to leave more frequently.

6. Overtime vs Attrition

Objective

Analyze the impact of overtime on attrition.

Insight

Employees working overtime exhibit a significantly higher attrition rate.

7. Job Satisfaction vs Attrition

Objective

Evaluate employee satisfaction.

Insight

Lower job satisfaction is associated with increased employee attrition.

8. Attrition by Years Since Last Promotion

Objective

Study promotion history.

Insight

Employees with longer periods since their last promotion demonstrate higher attrition.

9. Employee Career Progression

Objective

Categorize employees into career stages.

Insight

Early-career employees represent the largest workforce segment.

10. Education Field vs Attrition

Objective

Analyze attrition across education fields.

Insight

Life Sciences and Medical education fields contribute the largest number of employee exits.

11. Department Ranking by Attrition

Objective

Compare departments based on employee attrition.

Insight

Research & Development ranks first in employee attrition.
